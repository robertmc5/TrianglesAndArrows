Triangles, and therefore pointer or arrow-shaped structures can be made with some CSS tricks.

This CSS trick can be seen explained online at https://css-tricks.com/snippets/css/css-triangle/


The gist of it is to make a border around no content. {It's just a border around a singularity.}

When one side of the border is made transparent, the color of the connecting side seems to angle inward toward the center.

Kinda like a picture frame side piece around a picture.


If the top and bottom border colors are transparent, and the left border color isn't, then it appears as a triangle pointing to the right.

Whereas if the left and right border colors are transparent, and the bottom border color isn't, then it appears as a triangle pointing upward.

And so forth.
